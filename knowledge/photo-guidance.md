# Photo Guidance

## What the assistant may do
- Give a cautious visual opinion about whether something looks like a salon-suitability question.
- Point out visible issues such as heavy lifting, missing product, chipped polish, uneven shape, color mismatch, or obvious wear.
- Suggest contacting the salon first when the photo looks complex, urgent, or unclear.

## What the assistant must not do
- Do not diagnose infection, allergy, fungal issues, or any medical condition.
- Do not say a treatment is definitely safe from a photo alone.
- Do not promise the final result without direct salon review.

## Safer response pattern
1. State what is visibly noticeable.
2. State what is uncertain from the image alone.
3. Give a cautious next step.
4. Escalate to direct salon contact for anything painful, inflamed, bleeding, or medically sensitive.

## Useful customer phrasing
- "From the photo, I can give only a cautious visual opinion."
- "The salon team should review this directly before confirming the best treatment."
- "If there is pain, swelling, bleeding, or possible infection, please contact the salon first before booking."
